Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International

International Student Competition

Project

My love of flying has also led me to volunteer with a BYU competition team called the Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International (AUVSI). In this competition, a team is assigned an area to perform certain autonomous search and rescue tasks such as flying, searching for a lost hiker, and deciphering signs that the lost hiker left. The competition measures how well the aircraft can perform each task.

For this competition, I worked extensively on the image classification team, which focused on autonomously deciphering signs by shapes, letters, and colors, which the competition judges placed in unknown locations throughout the field. The system that I created was designed to fly the aircraft through a search pattern while simultaneously using a feature location method to locate and unscramble the message. I primarily used two networks, one called a spatial transformer network, which focused on locating the target, and a standard classification deep learning network to classify the images. My research on these topics serves as an invaluable foundation for the competition team in future years. Additionally, my experience with this competition has prepared me for my future work with SUAS research.
